About

I grew up fascinated by how living systems work — and frustrated by how slowly medicine moves. That gap is what drives my research: building the ML systems that close it.

The kind of researcher
who ships to production.

At UPMC's Chandra Lab, I'm leading the computational analysis of one of the most comprehensive protein panels available — the SomaScan 5K — across more than 5,000 patients in the COPDGene and SPIROMICS cohorts, looking for the molecular fingerprints that predict who will respond to treatment for emphysema.

Before CMU, I spent two years at HealSeq / IISc Bangalore building clinical AI tools now actively being tested in hospitals — a TB decision-support system fusing imaging, genomics, and socioeconomic data; a pulmonary platform that listens to cough sounds and reads chest X-rays. I care about going from research paper to real patient impact.

"Good science should improve someone's life. If it stays in a paper, it's not finished yet."
